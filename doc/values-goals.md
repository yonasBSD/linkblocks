# ties' Values and Goals

If you want to find out whether you should try out ties, this document might give you an idea of what to expect over the long term.

## Quality over Quantity

We aim to make ties delightful to use, build and maintain.

From the user's side, the UI should be fast, feel responsive, and easy to use.
It should be obvious how to do simple things, and there should be well-written instructions on how to do complex things.
Non-critical features that can't be made to work according to our values are removed, or not implemented at all.

Hosting and administrating a ties instance should be as simple as possible.
The amount of moderation required should be as close to zero as possible.

Default settings aim to strike a balance between security, predictability and ease of use.

From the software developer's side, we integrate and document tooling to make common workflows efficient and comfortable.
We take the time needed to understand, document, test and refactor the code so it's easy to work with in the future.

## Longevity & Robustness

We prioritize longevity over speed.
The goal is to maintain ties at least for the next 10 years, and make it easy for others to take over if needed.

Dependencies are carefully vetted for their maintenance status, robustness, and replaceability.

Errors and changes in ties' environment should be handled gracefully.

Leaning on backwards-compatible technology such as HTML, RSS, ActivityPub ensures that ties will remain useful even if new versions of these technologies come out.

## Non-goals

ties is made by the community, for the community.
Raising financial support to sustain the livelihood of its developers increases the projects longevity.
Making a profit, making changes only to increase its user base, or accepting any other commercial influence is against our values.

ties has quite a specific vision, and this means it won't fit everyone's needs.
This is fine, and feature requests that don't align with our vision or goals will be rejected, encouraging folks to use different software or build their own.

ties is not made to be a "town square" for public discussions.
Interaction between users will always be enabled by consent, not by moderation after the fact.
